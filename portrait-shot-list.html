Two portraits · One session

A matched pair —
Anthony & Giuseppe.

Two modern editorial portraits captured back-to-back in a single afternoon at the bakery. Same photographer, same lighting setup, same visual language. The only modern people-photography on the site — they need to feel like they belong in the same publication.

Style reference. Garden & Gun — Southern heritage portraiture, working-environment candor. Not corporate-headshot style. Real environment, working clothes, candid-but-considered.

Location. Inside the bakery at 2512 N. 15th Street, Ybor City. Available surroundings: stone hearth, wooden bench, proofing trays, front counter, delivery rack.

Light. Soft warm window light + ambient hearth/bakery glow. Color, modern, high-resolution.

Color. Honey-warm midtones, deep but not crushed shadows. Bakery interior softly out of focus — background environment can stay warm-cool mixed for cinematic depth.

Delivery. Graded JPGs at 2× target display size. No need for RAW or TIFF.

Session Logic

  • One Afternoon Both portraits captured in the same visit — minimize setup, maintain visual continuity.
  • Two Subjects Anthony Copeland Moré (4th-gen owner) & Giuseppe Bua (National Sales Manager).
  • One Lighting Setup Soft warm window key, gentle ambient fill. Carry across both subjects.
  • Releases Model releases signed at start of session.

Production Note

The two faces need to look like they belong in the same magazine spread.

Schedule Anthony & Giuseppe together. One session, two portraits — saves a half-day and gives the site a coherent human voice.
