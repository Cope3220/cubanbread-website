## 1 · Project Context

La Segunda Central Bakery has baked authentic Cuban bread in Ybor City, Tampa, since 1915 — passed down four generations from founder Juan Moré to current owner Anthony Copeland Moré. The new wholesale website at cubanbread.com is the bakery's first dedicated foodservice presence, distinct from its retail site.

The site spans five pages — Home, Our Story, Products, Distribution, and Contact. The visual language is **dark editorial heritage**: deep ink and maroon backgrounds, warm cream type, sparing red and gold accents. Photography carries the entire emotional weight of the design — typography is restrained, layouts are gridded, and there are no decorative illustrations. **Every image must earn its place.**

Three modes appear across the site:

- **Archival** — sepia / B&W heritage from family records
- **Process & environmental** — warm cinematic bakery interiors
- **Modern editorial portraits** — Anthony Moré, Giuseppe Bua

All three must feel like they belong in the same publication.

### Reference vocabulary

- **Cereal** — restraint, deep negative space, ingredient as protagonist
- **Garden & Gun** — Southern heritage portraiture, working-environment candor
- **Eater** — documentary process, motion in real kitchens, real heat, real hands

### Tonal direction

- **Archival** — whatever the source. De-noise only.
- **Process** — warm, candle-amber. High contrast.
- **Portraits** — soft window light, environmental, working clothes.
- **Product** — single warm key, deep falloff. Vermeer with flour.

## 2 · Color Science

Photography is graded to a five-color brand system established in the 2019 La Segunda Identity Guide. Capture cleanly; **grade to brand in post.** A LUT will be supplied in the production package; the swatches below are the targets it pulls toward.

### Brand palette

| Token | Hex | Reference |
|---|---|---|
| More Maroon | `#6D0020` | PMS 188 |
| Oven Red | `#EE3525` | PMS Bright Red |
| Cuban Gold | `#E5D660` | PMS 458 |
| Crust | `#9D6B3F` | Warm secondary |
| Ink | `#0d0d0d` | Site background |

### Capture targets

- **White Balance** — 2900–3400 K · warm tungsten bias for bakery interiors. Match to oven glow, not to fluorescents.
- **Exposure** — protect highlights on crust glaze and eggwash; let shadows fall to true black. The site background is `#0d0d0d` — shadow-falloff product shots will composite cleanly.
- **Color Bias** — push midtones toward warm amber/honey. Avoid magenta in skin (warm Hispanic/Mediterranean tones must read truthfully — neutral or honey-warm, never pink).
- **Crust Targets** — Cuban bread crust should grade to `#9D6B3F` in midtone, with golden specular highlights up to `#E5D660`.
- **Black Point** — shadow detail in modern frames is desirable but not mandatory; the design accommodates true-black shadow blocks. Do not lift the toe.

### Grading notes

- **Archival** — treat to either sepia or pure B&W. *Do not* add fake grain or vintage filters to modern shots — they will fight the real archival material.
- **Process / Hearth** — maroon-undertone shadows (lift to ≈`#1a0e08`, not pure black). Warm amber midtones. Highlights warm-gold, never cool-white.
- **Portraits** — honey-warm midtones, deep but not crushed shadows. Bakery interior softly out of focus — background environment can stay warm-cool mixed for cinematic depth.
- **Product** — highest contrast on the page. Single warm key, allow the shadow side to fall to ink. Negative space clean enough to silhouette-mask if needed.
- **Delivery** — 16-bit TIFF or full-range RAW + a graded JPEG/PNG preview at 2× target display size.

## 10 · Delivery Specs

- **File format.** RAW masters + 16-bit TIFF graded. JPG/PNG previews at 2× target display dimensions for web review.
- **Naming convention.** `laseg_shotNN_subject_v01.ext` — e.g. `laseg_shot19_anthony-more_v01.tif`, `laseg_shot32_marble_v01.tif`.
- **Color grading.** A LUT will be supplied with the production package targeting the 2019 Identity Guide palette. Capture cleanly, grade to brand. *Do not* hand-tint modern frames to fake archival look — the real archival material on the same page will fight it.
- **Releases.** Anthony Moré and Giuseppe Bua sign model releases at the portrait session. Anonymous bakers in process / loading-dock frames signed at the bakery shoot start. Photographer to provide release templates.
- **Review cycle.** Selects within 5 business days. Final delivery within 10 business days of shoot completion. Archival research is a separate workstream — no fixed deadline; quality over speed.
