§ 01Principles

Four anchors that hold every decision.

Our brand is a third-generation Tampa bakery operating at national wholesale scale. Every page, page section, and pixel should feel like it was made by people who have been doing this since 1915 — and intend to keep doing it.

01 / Heritage

Earned, not staged.

Lead with dates, names, and provenance — Juan Moré, Ybor City, the palmetto split. Never invent nostalgia.

02 / Plainspoken

The foreman's voice.

Direct, specific, never breezy. We talk to chefs, GMs, and buying agents — not to "guests."

03 / Editorial

Read it like a broadsheet.

Serifs, generous rules, restrained color. Layouts borrow from periodicals, not from SaaS marketing pages.

04 / Operational

Built to be quoted.

Every page surface has to support pack counts, lead times, distributor lists, and SKUs without losing its dignity.

§ 02Marks & Lockups

The palmetto sun,
and the wordmark beneath it.

Two assets carry the brand: the palmetto-leaf sun mark (gold rays with a red palmetto leaf), and the La Segunda wordmark in Playfair Display. They appear together on hero surfaces and split apart for compact contexts (favicon, social avatars, small print).

Primary lockup · centered, stacked, on ink

Symbolism

The rays reference the palmetto leaf — Florida's state tree and the natural fiber whose serrated edge was historically pressed into the dough to score each loaf. The red core honors the original 1915 maroon storefront sign in Ybor City.

Heritage range

The mark predates the wholesale operation by a century. Treat it like a maker's mark — small, deliberate, and unchanged. Never re-color, re-draw, or modernize the rays.

Primary lockup

Icon stacked above wordmark, centered, on ink (#0d0d0d) or cream (#F5F1E8). The horizontal lockup is reserved for the site navigation bar.

Clear space & minimums

Give the mark room to breathe.

Reserve a clear space equal to the width of one ray (X) on every side of the lockup. Never crop a ray, place type within the keep-out, or composite the mark onto a busy photograph.

  • ✓ DoPlace on ink, cream, paper, or maroon
  • ✓ DoPair icon & wordmark in stacked lockup on heroes
  • ✓ DoUse icon-only at 24–44px in nav and favicons
  • × Don'tRecolor the rays or palmetto leaf
  • × Don'tPlace on the Oven Red surface (clashes)
  • × Don'tApply drop shadows, glows, or strokes
§ 03Color System

Five identity colors,
two neutral grounds.

The palette is taken directly from the 1919 La Segunda Identity Guide. Charcoal ink and cream paper form the structural ground; Maroon, Oven Red, Cuban Gold, Pewter, and Crust appear in carefully rationed measure. Saturation is restrained — no fluorescents, no synthetic gradients.

§ 05Spacing & Surfaces

A four-pixel grid
and a single rule weight.

Spacing follows a 4px base scale; section padding sits at 120px desktop / 80px mobile. Hairline rules (1px) divide editorial blocks; the heavier 2px gold rule is reserved for pull quotes and section caps. Radii are restrained — premium hardware, not consumer software.

§ 06Components

The vocabulary of the site
— buttons, fields, cards, FAQ.

Every component is drawn from the live cubanbread.com pages so the system stays in sync. Buttons are clean rectangles with monospace labels at 11px / 0.22em — never playful, never pill-shaped.

Primary buttons

Oven Red on dark, ink on light. Monospace label, 11px / 0.22em tracking, slight ease on hover with arrow micro-shift.

How do I get wholesale pricing?
+

Reach out to Giuseppe Bua, our National Sales Manager, at the wholesale desk. We respond within 24 hours, no portal and no scripts — just an actual pricing sheet for your case-pack and lead time.

Is the bread frozen or fresh-daily?
+

Both. National distribution ships frozen, thaw-and-serve through Sysco, US Foods, PFG, GFS, Ben E. Keith, and Cheney Brothers. Fresh-daily delivery is available throughout the Tampa Bay area.

§ 07Imagery & Texture

Photograph the work,
not the lifestyle.

Imagery is documentary, not staged. We photograph the bakery floor at dawn, hands on dough, the palmetto split, archival family material — and let those images carry the heritage. No food-styling tropes. No people pointing at laptops.

Hero · loaves on bench
raking morning light
A · Production heroes

Full-bleed images on ink. Shot at the actual Ybor City line — dough, peels, deck ovens. Always available in 16:9 and 4:5 crops.

Portrait · the baker
or sales lead
B · People & portraits

Tight, three-quarter portraits on a dark backdrop. Subject named in caption — Giuseppe, the Moré family, the foreman.

Archival · 1920s bakery
or Ybor street scene
C · Heritage & archive

Family photographs, original storefront, vintage labels. Treat with the antique paper mat (see below) — never run them flush to ink.

Antique mat · 4:5 portrait

Antique photograph treatment

Mount heritage imagery on paper, not ink.

Archival photographs sit on the Paper ·02 ground (#EFEAD9), with a subtle vignette and a 45° hairline weave to suggest a printed press surface. Add a thin pewter caption underneath — date, place, photographer if known.

This treatment is reserved for genuinely old material. Modern photography uses the full-bleed ink hero or cream three-quarter crop — never the antique mat.

§ 08Voice & Copy

Talk to the foreman,
not the marketing team.

We write the way Giuseppe answers the wholesale phone: short, specific, and confident in the work. Heritage is invoked through evidence — dates, names, pallets, addresses — not adjectives. We never call the bread "artisanal" or "handcrafted."

◦ Voice · we say

  • "Baked in Ybor City since 1915."
  • "Talk to Giuseppe. Pricing in 24 hours."
  • "Frozen, thaw-and-serve, shipped through Sysco."
  • "Same family. Same recipe. Same palmetto split."
  • "Three generations on the same bench."

× Voice · we don't say

  • "Artisanal, small-batch Cuban bread."
  • "Crafted with love in the heart of Tampa."
  • "Elevate your guest experience."
  • "Authentic flavors from the streets of Havana."
  • "Bread reimagined for the modern menu."
